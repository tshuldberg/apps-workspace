## 2. Bowel Protocol — Day-by-Day Escalation Calendar

**Context:** Patient is on oxycodone (opioid analgesic) for post-surgical pain. Opioids cause constipation. This protocol is a CONDITIONAL escalation — each step is added only if no bowel movement has occurred. Steps are cumulative (earlier steps continue when adding new ones).

### Escalation Schedule

| Day at Home | Date | Day of Week | Action | Medications Active |
|-------------|------|-------------|--------|--------------------|
| Day 1 | Feb 11 | Tuesday | **START:** Docusate sodium with senna (Sennakot-S) — 1 tablet orally, twice daily, with plenty of fluids | Sennakot-S 1 tab 2x/day |
| Day 2 | Feb 12 | Wednesday | **If no BM:** ADD polyethylene glycol (Miralax) — 1 capful mixed in 6-8 oz of fluids, every morning | Sennakot-S 2x/day + Miralax AM |
| Day 3 | Feb 13 | Thursday | **If no BM:** ADD bisacodyl (Dulcolax) suppositories — 1 rectally every 12 hours | Sennakot-S 2x/day + Miralax AM + Dulcolax q12h |
| Day 4 | Feb 14 | Friday | **If no BM:** ADD mineral oil enema — administered rectally | Sennakot-S 2x/day + Miralax AM + Dulcolax q12h + mineral oil enema |
| Day 5 | Feb 15 | Saturday | **If all measures fail:** CALL Neurosurgery nurse at **(858) 554-8920** | All of above + phone call |

### Important Bowel Protocol Rules

1. **Conditional, not automatic:** Each escalation step is ONLY added if the patient has NOT had a bowel movement. If a bowel movement occurs at any point, do NOT add the next escalation step.
2. **Cumulative:** When escalating, you keep all previous steps AND add the new one. You do not replace earlier steps.
3. **De-escalation:** Once regular bowel movements resume, the escalation medications (Miralax, Dulcolax, mineral oil enema) can be stopped. However, Sennakot-S should CONTINUE as long as oxycodone is being taken.
4. **Hydration:** Adequate fluid intake is critical for the stool softener/laxative to work. "Plenty of fluids" is emphasized in both the PDF and the Regimen.
5. **Duration of baseline (Sennakot-S):** Continue Sennakot-S for the entire duration of opioid therapy. When oxycodone is discontinued, Sennakot-S can be stopped.

## 3. Pantoprazole Duration Note

**Medication:** Pantoprazole (Protonix) 40 mg
**From PDF:** "FOR STOMACH ACIDS WHILE ON STEROID"
**From Regimen.docx:** Listed as a scheduled medication (morning timing)

### Duration Guidance

Pantoprazole is prescribed specifically as gastric protection during steroid therapy. Steroids (dexamethasone) increase the risk of gastric irritation and peptic ulcers, especially when combined with other medications.

**Minimum duration:** Pantoprazole should continue AT LEAST through **February 17, 2026** (the last day of dexamethasone).

**Recommended stop date:** **February 18, 2026** — the day after the last dexamethasone dose. Since the steroid will still have some residual effect on Feb 17 (the drug's biological half-life extends beyond the dosing window), continuing pantoprazole through the end of that day and stopping the following morning is reasonable.

**Summary:** Pantoprazole tracks with dexamethasone. When dexamethasone ends (after Feb 17 dose), pantoprazole can be discontinued the next day (Feb 18). If there is any doubt, confirm with the prescribing provider.

## 4. Discrepancies and Flags

### Discrepancy A: Missing External Calendar
The Regimen.docx states:
> "dexAMETHasone (DECADRON) -- see separate 'timeanddate calendar' for regimen, take after meals"

**Issue:** This referenced "timeanddate calendar" was NOT provided among the discharge materials audited. The taper schedule in this audit is derived from the official PDF discharge instructions, which contain the explicit step-down dosing. The external calendar may contain the same information in a visual format, but its absence means we cannot cross-verify.

**Risk:** LOW — the PDF instructions are authoritative and complete. The external calendar is likely a visual aid, not a separate prescription.

### Discrepancy B: Bowel Protocol Omitted from Regimen
The Regimen.docx lists only:
> "Sennakot-S -- plenty of fluids" (morning and evening)

**Issue:** The full bowel protocol escalation (Miralax -> Dulcolax suppositories -> mineral oil enema -> call neurosurgery) is present in the official PDF but is **completely absent** from the Regimen.docx.

**Risk:** HIGH — If the patient or caregiver relies solely on the Regimen.docx, they will not know about the escalation steps. They may endure multiple days of constipation without knowing that additional interventions are prescribed and available, or that they should call the neurosurgery nurse if all measures fail.

**Recommendation:** The bowel protocol escalation steps MUST be incorporated into the master medication schedule. This has been flagged as a critical gap.

### Discrepancy C: Conditional vs. Automatic Escalation
**Clarification:** The bowel protocol is explicitly conditional. The PDF states escalation with "If no bowel movement by [day]..." language. This means:
- If the patient has a bowel movement on Day 1 from Sennakot-S alone, there is NO need to add Miralax on Day 2
- Each step is only triggered by the absence of a bowel movement
- This is NOT a fixed schedule like the dexamethasone taper — it requires daily assessment

**Risk:** MEDIUM — A caregiver unfamiliar with escalation protocols might either (a) add everything on Day 1 (over-treatment) or (b) not escalate at all because the Regimen doesn't mention it (under-treatment). Clear labeling of the conditional nature is important.

### Discrepancy D: Pantoprazole Stop Date Not Specified
**Issue:** Neither the PDF nor the Regimen.docx specifies when to stop pantoprazole. The PDF only says it is "FOR STOMACH ACIDS WHILE ON STEROID," implying it should stop when the steroid stops, but this is not made explicit.

**Recommendation:** The medication schedule should note that pantoprazole ends on or after February 18, 2026 (the day after the last dexamethasone dose).
