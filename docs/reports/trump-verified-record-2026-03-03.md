# 1. CRIMINAL CONVICTIONS AND INDICTMENTS

## 1.1 People of New York v. Donald J. Trump -- Manhattan Hush Money Case
- **Charges:** 34 felony counts of falsifying business records (NY Penal Law Section 175.10)
- **Evidence:** Trump directed attorney Michael Cohen to pay $130,000 to Stormy Daniels before the 2016 election. Reimbursements were falsely recorded as "legal expenses." Testimony from Cohen, Daniels, David Pecker (National Enquirer), Hope Hicks, and documentary evidence.
- **Verdict:** GUILTY on all 34 counts (May 30, 2024). First sitting or former US president convicted of criminal charges.
- **Sentence:** Unconditional discharge (January 10, 2025). No prison, fines, or probation.
- **Status:** Conviction stands. Under appeal to 2nd Circuit.
- Sources: Manhattan DA, NPR, PBS

## 1.2 United States v. Donald J. Trump -- Classified Documents (S.D. Florida)
- **Charges:** 40 counts total: 32 counts willfully retaining national defense information, 5 counts obstruction, 1 count interfering with federal investigation, 4 counts false statements.
- **Evidence:** Highly classified documents (including nuclear program materials) found at Mar-a-Lago. Allegations of deleted security footage and false statements to investigators.
- **Outcome:** DISMISSED (July 15, 2024) by Judge Aileen Cannon (Trump appointee) on Appointments Clause grounds. Dismissed without prejudice.
- **Status:** DOJ dropped appeal after Trump won 2024 election (policy against prosecuting sitting presidents).
- Sources: DOJ, AP, Reuters

## 1.3 United States v. Donald J. Trump -- January 6 Election Obstruction (D.C.)
- **Charges:** 4 counts: conspiracy to defraud the US, conspiracy against rights, conspiracy to commit wire fraud, corruptly obstructing an official proceeding.
- **Evidence:** Knowingly false fraud claims, pressure on state officials, fraudulent elector slates, pressure on VP Pence.
- **Outcome:** DISMISSED without prejudice (December 6, 2024). DOJ cited policy against prosecuting sitting president. Prosecutors stated dismissal was "not a reflection of the merit of the cases."
- Sources: DOJ, NPR, Washington Post

## 1.4 State of Georgia v. Donald J. Trump et al. -- Fulton County RICO
- **Charges:** 8 felony counts including racketeering under Georgia RICO statute (max 20 years), solicitation of violation of oath by public officer, conspiracy to commit forgery, false statements. 18 co-defendants.
- **Evidence:** January 2, 2021 phone call to Raffensperger ("find 11,780 votes"), fraudulent elector slates, coordinated pressure campaign.
- **Outcome:** ALL CHARGES DISMISSED (November 26, 2025). DA Fani Willis disqualified over relationship with special prosecutor. Replacement prosecutor filed for dismissal.
- Sources: Fulton County Superior Court, AP, CNN

## 1.5 Trump Organization Criminal Tax Fraud (Manhattan DA)
- **Charges:** 17 felony counts against Trump Corporation and Trump Payroll Corporation: criminal tax fraud, conspiracy, falsifying business records. (Trump personally was NOT charged.)
- **Scheme:** 15-year tax evasion (2005-2021). CFO Allen Weisselberg evaded taxes on $1.76 million in unreported compensation (free apartment, Mercedes leases, tuition).
- **Verdict:** GUILTY on all 17 counts (December 6, 2022).
- **Penalty:** $1.6 million fine (maximum allowed). Weisselberg: 5 months jail, 5 years probation, ~$2 million in back taxes/penalties.
- **Status:** CONVICTION FINAL. Weisselberg later pled guilty to perjury (2024) in separate case.
- Sources: Manhattan DA, ABC News

# 2. MAJOR CIVIL JUDGMENTS AND SETTLEMENTS

## 2.1 NY Attorney General v. Trump Organization -- Civil Fraud ($464M Judgment)
- **Filed:** September 21, 2022 by AG Letitia James
- **Scheme:** 2011-2021, 200+ false asset valuations on financial statements.
- **Key inflations:**
  - Trump Tower penthouse: Claimed 30,000 sq ft; actual 10,996 sq ft. Valued at $327M.
  - Mar-a-Lago: Valued at $739M as residential development; deed restricts it to social club use.
  - 40 Wall Street: Valued at $527M; independent appraisal $220M.
  - Trump Park Avenue rent-stabilized units: Claimed ~$50M; bank appraised at $750K.
- **Judgment (Feb 16, 2024):** Justice Engoron found fraud "so clear" no jury needed. Ordered $464.5 million. Trump banned from serving as officer/director of any NY company for 3 years.
- **Appeal (Aug 21, 2025):** Appellate Division UPHELD fraud liability but VOIDED the $464M penalty as excessive. AG James appealing.
- Sources: NY AG, Politico, NY Supreme Court records

## 2.2 E. Jean Carroll v. Donald J. Trump -- Sexual Abuse and Defamation
- **Trial I (May 2023):** Jury found Trump LIABLE for sexual abuse and defamation. Awarded $5 million.
- **Trial II (January 2024):** Jury awarded $83.3 million for defamation ($18.3M compensatory + $65M punitive).
- **Total across both trials:** $88.3 million (with interest, exceeds $89.7 million).
- **Evidence:** Carroll's testimony about 1996 assault at Bergdorf Goodman. Corroborating testimony from other women. "Access Hollywood" tape.
- **Appeals:** Second Circuit AFFIRMED both verdicts (Dec 30, 2024 and Sept 8, 2025). SCOTUS cert petition pending.
- Sources: SDNY, 2nd Circuit opinions, NPR, CNN

## 2.3 Trump University -- $25 Million Settlement
- **Operated:** 2005-2010. For-profit "real estate training" ($1,495 to $34,995 per course).
- **Fraud:** Falsely claimed Trump was involved in curriculum. Students paid up to $60K for generic content.
- **Settlement (finalized Jan 31, 2018):** $25 million. ~90% recovery for 6,000+ students. No admission of wrongdoing.
- Sources: S.D. California court records, ABC News

## 2.4 Trump Foundation -- Court-Ordered Dissolution
- **Key findings:**
  - No board meeting since 1999 (legal requirement violated).
  - Used foundation funds to settle Trump business lawsuits.
  - Trump bought a $10,000 portrait of himself with foundation money.
  - Campaign staff directed foundation grants to politically favorable charities.
- **Outcome (Nov 2019):** Trump ordered to pay $2 million in damages. Required to make 19 admissions of personal misuse. Foundation dissolved under court supervision (Dec 2018).
- Sources: NY AG, NY Supreme Court

- **What he DID:** Called Senator Tommy Tuberville about continuing electoral objections while the attack was underway. Issued a few tweets (some only at repeated urging of daughter Ivanka and other advisors).
- **What he DID NOT do:** Did not call law enforcement. Did not call the Secretary of Defense. Did not call the Attorney General. Did not call the Secretary of Homeland Security. Did not call the National Guard.
- **2:24 PM:** Tweeted attacking Mike Pence: "Mike Pence didn't have the courage to do what should have been done."
- **4:17 PM:** Finally posted video telling rioters to go home but added: "We love you. You're very special." And repeated: "the election was stolen from us."

Sources: [CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2022/07/20/politics/what-we-learned-trump-187-minutes), [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2022/07/22/1112323797/jan-6-hearing-recap-187-minutes), [Washington Post](https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/interactive/2021/what-happened-trump-jan-6-insurrection/)
