# High-Protein Smoothie Recipes (No Protein Powder)

> **Source:** [EatingWell — 13 High-Protein Smoothie Recipes Without Protein Powder](https://www.eatingwell.com/no-protein-powder-high-protein-smoothie-recipes-11812756)
> All recipes contain 15g+ protein per serving from whole foods: Greek yogurt, kefir, soy milk, hemp seeds, peanut butter, chia seeds, and almonds.

|---|---|---|---|

| Frozen strawberries | ~1.5 cups | 1 bag (16 oz) | 8-12 months |
| Frozen blueberries | 3/4 cup | 1 bag (12 oz) | 8-12 months |
| Frozen mixed berries | 1.5 cups | 1 bag (16 oz) | 8-12 months |
| Frozen dark sweet cherries | 1/4 cup | 1 small bag (12 oz) | 8-12 months |
| Frozen banana chunks | ~6 bananas worth | Buy fresh, slice & freeze | 3-6 months |
| Diced seedless watermelon | 2 cups | Buy fresh, dice & freeze | 3-6 months in freezer |

### Freezer-Prep Strategy (For 2-Month Supply)

1. **Buy bananas in bulk** (12-15), let ripen, peel, slice into coins, freeze flat on a sheet pan, then bag.
2. **Buy all frozen fruit bags** at once — peaches, mango, strawberries, blueberries, mixed berries, cherries.
3. **Watermelon**: buy fresh, dice into cubes, freeze flat on sheet pan, then bag.
4. **Spinach**: buy 2 bags, portion into 1-cup amounts in freezer bags (works great in smoothies from frozen).
5. **Greek yogurt**: portion into ice cube trays (2 tbsp per cube), freeze, bag. Thaw slightly before blending.
6. **Kefir**: can be frozen in ice cube trays for up to 2 months (probiotic count may decrease slightly).
7. **Dates**: already shelf-stable for months, no freezing needed.

### 1. Watermelon-Peach Smoothie

| | |
|---|---|
| **Servings** | 2 |
| **Protein** | 16g per serving |
| **Calories** | 209 per serving |
| **Fat / Carbs** | 3g / 32g |
| **Time** | 5 minutes |

**Ingredients:**
- 2 cups diced seedless watermelon
- 2 cups frozen sliced peaches
- 1 1/4 cups low-fat plain Greek yogurt

**Instructions:** Blend all ingredients until smooth. Serve immediately.

**Notes:** Ultra-simple 3-ingredient recipe. Vegetarian, gluten-free. Greek yogurt is the sole protein source.

### 3. Mango-Almond Smoothie Bowl

| | |
|---|---|
| **Servings** | 1 |
| **Protein** | ~15g+ |
| **Time** | 10 minutes |

**Ingredients:**
- 1/2 cup frozen mango chunks
- 1/4 cup frozen banana slices
- 1/4 cup plain unsweetened almond milk
- 5 tbsp unsalted almonds (3 tbsp blended in, 2 tbsp for topping)
- 1/8 tsp ground allspice
- 1/4 cup raspberries (topping)
- 1/2 tsp honey (topping)

**Instructions:**
1. Blend mango, banana, almond milk, 3 tbsp almonds, and allspice until very smooth.
2. Pour into a bowl.
3. Top with raspberries, remaining 2 tbsp almonds, and honey.

**Notes:** Use frozen fruit (not fresh) to keep it thick and frosty. Almonds provide the protein. Originally published in EatingWell Magazine, Sept/Oct 2017.

### 4. No-Added-Sugar Mango Lassi Smoothie

| | |
|---|---|
| **Servings** | 1 |
| **Protein** | ~15g+ |
| **Time** | 5 minutes |

**Ingredients:**
- 1 Medjool date (pitted)
- 1 cup milk
- 1/4 cup plain Greek yogurt
- 1 cup frozen mango chunks
- 1/4 tsp ground cardamom
- 1 tbsp hemp seeds

**Instructions:** Place all ingredients in a blender. Process until smooth, about 1 minute, stopping to scrape down sides as needed.

**Notes:** Inspired by traditional Indian lassi. Sweetness comes entirely from mango and the date — zero added sugar. Greek yogurt + hemp seeds provide protein.

**Ingredients:**

### 6. Chocolate-Peanut Butter Protein Shake

| | |
|---|---|
| **Servings** | 1 (yields ~2 cups) |
| **Protein** | ~20g+ |
| **Time** | 5 minutes |

**Ingredients:**
- 1 cup vanilla soymilk (unsweetened)
- 1 frozen banana (chunked)
- 1/4 cup plain Greek yogurt
- 1 tbsp unsweetened cocoa powder
- 1 tbsp natural peanut butter

**Instructions:** Combine soymilk, banana chunks, Greek yogurt, cocoa powder, and peanut butter in a blender. Blend on high for about a minute until smooth. If too thick, add a splash more soymilk.

**Notes:** Triple protein source — soymilk, yogurt, and peanut butter. Tastes like a chocolate-PB banana milkshake. Use "natural" nut butter to avoid added trans fats, oils, or sugar. Add ingredients in the order listed for best consistency.

**Substitutions:** Any nut/seed butter works. Swap soymilk for almond, cashew, or dairy milk (unsweetened).

### 7. Strawberry-Peach Chia Seed Smoothie

| | |
|---|---|
| **Servings** | 2 (1.5 cups each) |
| **Protein** | 10g per serving |
| **Calories** | 247 per serving |
| **Fat / Carbs** | 7g / 41g |
| **Fiber** | 10g |
| **Time** | 5 minutes |

**Ingredients:**
- 1 1/2 cups frozen strawberries
- 1 cup frozen peach slices
- 1 cup unsweetened almond milk
- 1/2 cup reduced-fat plain Greek yogurt
- 1/4 cup frozen dark sweet cherries
- 3 dried pitted Medjool dates
- 2 tbsp chia seeds

**Instructions:** Place all ingredients in a blender. Process until smooth, about 30 seconds. Divide between 2 glasses and serve immediately.

**Notes:** Chia seeds expand with liquid, adding thickness and 10g fiber per serving. Dates provide natural sweetness. Sweet and tangy with a velvety texture.

## Notes

- Recipes 2, 5, 6, and 13 have estimated measurements based on EatingWell's consistent patterns across the collection; exact amounts could not be confirmed due to the site's paywall.
- Recipe 11 (Golden-Milk Turmeric) shows only 7g protein in its standalone version. Adding 1/4 cup Greek yogurt brings it to ~15g.
- For maximum protein, prioritize recipes 6 (Chocolate-PB, ~20g), 10 (Spinach-Avocado, 18g), 1 (Watermelon-Peach, 16g), and 9 (Spinach-PB-Banana, 16g).
- All frozen fruit can be purchased in bulk bags and stored 8-12 months, making the 2-month supply plan very feasible.
- Bananas are the only fruit best bought fresh and frozen at home (peel, slice, freeze flat, then bag).
